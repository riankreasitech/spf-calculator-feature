# spf.io Pricing & FAQ

This document summarizes the billing structure, pricing logic, and common questions discovered during research for the spf.io platform.

## 1. Pricing Structure Overview
Spf.io uses a hybrid model combining subscriptions and usage-based credits.

- **Subscription Model**: Most organizational users pay a monthly or annual fee.
    - **Estimated Entry Cost**: $99 - $199+ per month for small-to-midsize organizations.
- **Usage Minutes**: Every plan includes an allocation of "Usage Minutes" or "Subscription Hours." 
    - Minutes are consumed when the AI engines (captioning, translation, or text-to-speech) are active.
- **Overages**: Usage beyond the monthly quota is billed at a per-minute or per-hour rate.
- **Custom Quotes**: Required for large events, high-volume needs, or "human-in-the-loop" managed services.

## 2. Key Billing Variables
For a pricing calculator, the following factors are critical:
1. **Event Frequency**: One-time vs. Recurring (Monthly/Annual).
2. **Usage Volume**: Expected total hours of live/post-production content per month.
3. **Number of Target Languages**: Simultaneous translation into multiple languages may affect the tier or credit consumption rate.
4. **Service Level**: 
    - **Self-Service**: Fully automated AI captions and translation.
    - **Assisted**: Includes human oversight or professional captioners.
5. **Add-ons**: Custom AI models (vocabulary training) or specialized onboarding packages.

## 3. Training & Onboarding
- **Setup Fee**: Organizations often pay a one-time onboarding fee for portal setup and staff training.
- **Demo**: Users are encouraged to "Request a Demo" to get a customized price quote rather than seeing a public rate card.

## 4. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### What languages does spf.io support?
Spf.io supports real-time translation and captioning in **over 60 languages**.

### Does it work offline?
No. Spf.io is a **cloud-based platform** and requires a stable internet connection to communicate with its AI translation and speech engines.

### How do I view captions?
Attendees typically view captions on their own **smartphones or tablets** via a web-link (no app download required). It can also be projected onto large screens or integrated into live stream overlays.

### Can I correct the AI in real-time?
Yes. The **Director** tool allows a human to monitor the AI output and make instant corrections to names, technical terms, or errors before they are seen by the wider audience.

### Does it work with Zoom or YouTube?
Yes. There are dedicated integration flows for major meeting and streaming platforms (see `integrations.md` for technical details).
